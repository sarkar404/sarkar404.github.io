Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Electronics Engineering
Hochschule Bremen, Germany | March 2018 – March 2020

Dissertation:

This master’s thesis explores the innovative use of WLAN technology, leveraging the Channel State Information (CSI) from IEEE 802.11n to detect human presence and estimate breathing rates within vehicles. By analyzing amplitude and phase shifts in Wi‑Fi signals, the system accurately identifies motion and the periodic movements associated with breathing. Extensive tests in a Mercedes‑Benz E‑Class demonstrated its effectiveness across seating arrangements and transmitter‑receiver configurations. Potential applications include child‐presence detection, driver‐fatigue monitoring, and in‑vehicle health surveillance, offering a promising non‑invasive enhancement for automotive safety systems.

Bachelor of Engineering (B.E.) in Electrical (Telecommunication) Engineering
Sukkur IBA University | August 2011 – November 2015

Dissertation:

This bachelor’s thesis investigates the downlink performance of two‑tier cellular networks by employing stochastic geometry and advanced spatial models—Voronoi tessellations for cell structures and the Hard‑Core Point Process for base station placement. Three scenarios are analyzed: a single‑tier macrocell environment, a standard macro‑femto coexistence model, and an enhanced two‑tier design incorporating interference mitigation. The enhanced approach segments cells into center and edge regions, applies fractional frequency reuse, and uses adaptive power control to counteract interference. Simulation results demonstrate notable gains in coverage, capacity, and outage reduction, offering valuable guidance for resilient urban network deployments.
