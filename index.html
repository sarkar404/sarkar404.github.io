Perisens GmbH

Master Thesis Student

July 2019 - December 2019 --> 6 mos

Feldkirchen, Germany

  • "In-Vehicle Human Presence Detection and Breathing Rate Estimation using IEEE 802.11n Channel State Information"

    This master's thesis explores the innovative use of WLAN technology, particularly leveraging the Channel State Information (CSI) from IEEE 802.11n, to detect human presence and estimate breathing rates within vehicles. By analyzing the amplitude and phase shifts in Wi-Fi signals, the system accurately identifies motion and the periodic movements associated with breathing. Extensive tests conducted in a Mercedes-Benz E-Class vehicle demonstrated the system's effectiveness across various seating arrangements and transmitter-receiver configurations. The research offers potential applications in child presence detection, driver fatigue monitoring, and in-vehicle health monitoring, providing a promising non-invasive solution for enhancing automotive safety systems.
